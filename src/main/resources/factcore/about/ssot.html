Source Of Truth

A (Single) Source Of Truth (SSOT) refers to the practice of structuring data and meta-data so that every element is known by a single authoritative identity, stored in a single definitive location.

Scorpio4 implements a number of tools and strategies to assist with the creation, maintenance and exploitation of a SSOT

Any links or uses of an information element (possibly in a SQL schema, federated or exotic data stores) are by reference only.

Thus, when any such SSOT element is modified, the update propagates to the dependent systems throughout enterprise. Third party uses of the information are reference, or deterministically derived (inferred).

Creating an SSOT

  • Define definitions in a master data dictionary.
  • Collect the desired data.
  • link data elements together and to their definitions.
  • Measure and analyze the data against known standards and benchmarks.
  • Derive inter-dependent use cases such as physical schemas, business rules and user interfaces.

Master Data Management

Scorpio4 supports multiple models per business entity. A person is not one-dimensional. We are parents, friends, colleagues. With each party, we have different motivations and requirements.

Each element within the SSOT can be linked to any other, furthermore rich meta-data about the relationship can be captured too.

You should think in terms of aligning information entities to a model of engagement defined by the business. Allow for flexibility in different situations then capture and exploit the relationships between the data.

Scorpio4 employs an RDF/SPARQL repository to store data and meta-data about the enterprise, it's infrastructure and the integration between data, people and processes.

Systems Integration

A common approach to working with disparate systems is to employ a Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) that employs an Enterprise Service Bus (ESB).

A SOA/ESB allows services to interact and inter-operate without the traditional complexity, or strong coupling.

Knowledge Warehouse

A Knowledge Warehouse stores data and meta-data that has, or can be, reshaped and re-purposed to meet different business requirements.

A Knowledge Warehouse is fed from multiple systems, so it is not an SSOT but rather each data element refers to the SSOT.

The Knowledge Warehouse stores meta-data about your data environment. It can replicate data too making the repository a richly, query-friendly, self-describing storage engine.
